Carrot Cake

Slice of Carrot Cake topped with creamcheese frosting and chopped pecans

Description

This carrot cake is the ultimate cozy companion, featuring a moist, spice-infused crumb packed with toasted pecans and sweet, shredded carrots. Topped with a luscious layer of tangy cream cream frosting, it offers a beautiful balance of textures that makes every bite feel like a warm hug. Ingredients

This recipe yields 16 slices

Carrot Cake

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 Teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 Teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1 1/2 Teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/4 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 cups carrots, grated
  • 1 cup pecans, coarsely chopped
  • 1/2 cup raisins

Cream Cheese Frosting

  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 1 1/4 cups powered sugar
  • 1 container frozen whipped topping, thawed
  • 1/2 cup pecans, coarsely chopped

Steps

Bake Cake

  1. Heat oven to 350 F and grease two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, salt and cinnamon in a bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the oil, granulate sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla. Add the eggs one at a time.
  4. Gradually stir in the dry ingredients.
  5. Stir in carrots, nuts and raisins.
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the cake pans and bake for 35 - 45 minutes.
  7. ALlow the cakes to completely cool before attempting to put on the frosting.

Frost Cake

  1. Beat the cream cheese with a hand mixer until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the powered sugar one spoonful at a time.
  3. Fold in the whipped topping.
  4. Frost the top of the first cake. Stack the second cake on top and then frost the top of the final cake.
  5. Sprinkle the chopped pecans to finish.
