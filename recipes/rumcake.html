Rum Cake

Rum cake on serving dish

Description

This signature rum cake is a true piece of culinary history, boasting a rich, buttery cake and a deep, spirited glaze that only improves with time. More than just a dessert, this is a cherished heirloom recipe that has been passed down through our family line for generations, carrying with it the warmth of countless holiday celebrations and Sunday dinners. Each slice is a nostalgic journey, delivering a perfectly moist texture and a sophisticated sweetness that simply can't be replicated by modern shortcuts.

Ingredients

This recipe yields 16 slices

Cake

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 3.4 oz box instant vanilla pudding mix
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 8 Teaspoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 Teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 cup milk, room temperature
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup rum, plain or spiced
  • 2 Teaspoons pure vanilla extract

Syrup

  • 8 Tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 Teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup rum, plain or spiced
  • 1/2 Teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Steps

Making the Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 F.
  2. Combine the flour, sugar, pudding mix, baking powder, salt, butter, and vegetable oil in a mixing bowl.
  3. Beat in the milk and each egg individually.
  4. Stir in the rum and vanilla extract.
  5. Spritz a 10 - 12 cup bundt pan with cooking spray. Pour the batter into the pan and make sure it's level.
  6. Bake for 50 - 60 minutes.
  7. Remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool slightly.

Making the Syrup

  1. COmbine the butter, water, sugar, salt and rum in a saucepan.
  2. Heat to a rapid bowl then reduce to a simmer and cook (without stirring) for about 5 - 8 minutes.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Poke holes all over the cake with a long skewer and pour 1/4 cup of the syrup over the cake in phases until all the syrup is used.
  5. Cover the pan with plastic wrap and allow the cake to sit overnight at room temperature to soak in all the rum.
