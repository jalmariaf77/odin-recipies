Peach Cobbler

Bowl of peach cobbler with a spoon

Description

This peach cobbler is the ultimate way to end your day on a high note, featuring sun-ripened peaches bubbling in a spiced syrup beneath a golden, buttery biscuit crust. There’s something incredibly soothing about the contrast between the warm, jammy fruit and a cold scoop of vanilla bean ice cream melting over the top. It’s the kind of rustic, soul-warming dessert that invites you to slow down, grab a spoon, and savor every last bite of sweetness.

Ingredients

This recipe yields 9 servings

Crust

  • 1 cups all-purpose flour
  • 6 Teaspoons butter
  • 2 Teaspoons baking powder
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 Teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sugar
  • Ground cinnamon

Peach Filling

  • 5 peaches, peeled, cored and sliced
  • 3/4 cups sugar
  • 1 container frozen whipped topping, thawed
  • 1/4 Teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Add sliced peaches, sugar and salt to a pan and stir.
  2. Cook on medium heat until the sugar completely dissolves and then remove it from heat.
  3. Preheat oven to 350 F.Slice butter into pieces and add to the 9x13 inch baking dish. Melt the butter in the dish while the oven is preheating and then remove it.
  4. Combine the flour, sugar, baking powder and salt and then stir in milk.
  5. Pour the mixture into the baking dish, over the butter and smooth it into an even layer.
  6. Spoon the peaches and join over the batter and sprinkle cinnamon over the top.
  7. Bake at 350 F for 38 - 40 minutes. Serve warm with a scoop of ice cream!
