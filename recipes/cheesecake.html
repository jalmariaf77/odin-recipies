Cheesecake

Slice of cheesecake garnised with raspberries and a blackberry

Description

This show-stopping cheesecake is the ultimate centerpiece for any celebration, boasting a velvety-smooth texture and a buttery graham cracker crust that melts in your mouth. It has become a legendary staple in our circle, with family and friends requesting it at every single holiday without fail. Whether topped with a glossy fruit compote or served elegantly plain, its rich flavor and foolproof consistency make it the most anticipated dish on the dessert table.

Ingredients

This recipe yields 12 slices

Graham Cracker Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 2 Tablespoons sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon brown sugar
  • 7 Tablespoons butter, melted

Cheesecake

  • 32 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2/3 cups sour cream
  • 1 1/2 Teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/8 Teaspoons salt
  • 4 large eggs

Steps

  1. Preheat over to 325 F.
  2. Prepare the crust first by combining graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and brown sugar. Add melted butter and combine well.
  3. Press the crumbs into the bottom of the cheesecake pan. Set aside.
  4. Beat the cheesecake with a hand mixer in a large bowl until smooth.
  5. Add sugar and stir well.
  6. Add sour cream, vanilla extract, and salt and stir well.
  7. Slowly add each egg, pausing between each egg to make sure the mixture is mixed well.
  8. Pour the cheesecake mixer on top of the graham cracker crumb crust.
  9. Bake for 50 - 60 minutes on the middle rack. The edges will be puffed but the center will still be wiggle like Jello.
  10. Remove from the oven and allow to cool for 1 - 2 hours before transferring the dessert to the refridgerator.
  11. Eat yo treat!
